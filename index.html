AI-Native Practice

AI isn't a tool I reach for.
It's the environment I work in.

Every phase of my design process has an AI layer — not as a shortcut, but as a force multiplier for quality and speed. I distinguish between prompt-level usage (structured, multi-shot prompts with explicit constraints and format instructions for repeatable creative output) and token-efficient workflow design (persistent system prompts, batched tasks, and cumulative project context so AI collaboration scales across complex, multi-month engagements without drift or setup overhead).

Research & Synthesis

Interview synthesis at speed and coverage

Structured prompts to analyse transcripts, cluster themes, and generate insight hierarchies from raw user data. What used to take a full day of affinity mapping now takes an hour — and surfaces patterns I'd have missed by hand.

Prompt Engineering

Multi-shot prompts with format constraints

I write prompts the way I write design specs — with explicit output format, examples, edge cases, and constraints. Consistent quality across every call. No re-explaining context. No lottery output. Repeatable at scale across 110+ journey projects.

Token-Efficient Workflow Design

System prompts that carry project memory

Persistent system prompts that front-load design system vocabulary, component rules, and project constraints. Every AI call is informed by cumulative context — not a blank slate. This is what makes AI sustainable on long enterprise projects, not just good for one-off tasks.

LLM Product Design

Designing UX for AI features themselves

Conversation flows, uncertainty states, progressive disclosure of AI recommendations, feedback loops for model improvement. Cozy Nights' Intellisleep adaptation algorithm — and how a user interacts with a system optimising their sleep while they're unconscious — is one worked example.

About

The designer
behind the work

I'm a product designer who works at the intersection of systems thinking, enterprise complexity, and human-centred design. My background spans a B.Tech in Information Technology and an M.Des from IIT Delhi — which means I'm equally comfortable talking to engineers about API constraints and to business stakeholders about regulatory requirements. I'm also fully AI-native: structured prompt engineering, token-efficient multi-session workflows, and LLM product design are part of how I work, not tools I occasionally pick up.

Most of my career has been spent solving problems that don't have clean boundaries — multi-channel journeys spanning apps, web portals, and physical bank branches simultaneously; insurance products requiring clearance from 11 internal SBI teams before a single screen is frozen; a design system holding together across 65 designers and 110+ journeys. I also contributed to a VR research project for the Indian Armed Forces under IIT Delhi — a proof-of-concept later presented to the President of India.

Outside of product work, I mentor CEED aspirants — 35+ mentees have been selected into IITs and NIDs. I believe good design decisions can always be explained clearly, and that the best work comes from being inside a problem long enough to understand it completely.
